## 2. What are Suggested Buckets?

### Definition

A **Suggested Bucket** is a pre-configured investment portfolio containing 2-5 carefully selected mutual funds with specific allocation percentages (weightages). Each bucket is designed for a particular investment goal and risk profile.

### Components of a Bucket

1. **Portfolio Name**: Descriptive name indicating the bucket's purpose
2. **Description**: Brief explanation of the investment strategy
3. **Risk Level**: 
   - **Low**: Conservative, stable returns
   - **Moderate**: Balanced risk and returns
   - **High**: Aggressive growth potential
4. **Category**: 
   - **Investment**: For wealth creation goals
   - **Retirement**: For retirement planning
   - **Both**: Suitable for both purposes
5. **Fund Selection**: 2-5 mutual funds with specific weightages
6. **Performance Metrics**: Historical performance data and projections

### Example Bucket Structure

```
Bucket Name: "Conservative Growth Portfolio"
Description: "Low-risk portfolio for stable long-term growth"
Risk Level: Low
Category: Investment
Funds:
  - Fund A: 40% allocation
  - Fund B: 35% allocation
  - Fund C: 25% allocation
Total: 100%
```

## 6. Using Suggested Buckets

### Importing to Investment Plan

**Step 1**: Click the **"Import to Investment"** button on the bucket card

**Step 2**: You'll be automatically navigated to the Investment Plan page

**Step 3**: The bucket's funds are automatically loaded with their weightages

**Step 4**: You can now:
- Use any of the Investment calculators (SIP, Lumpsum, SIP+Lumpsum)
- Adjust investment amounts
- Set date ranges
- Calculate returns based on your specific parameters

**Step 2**: You'll be automatically navigated to the Retirement Plan page

**Step 3**: The bucket's funds are automatically loaded with their weightages

**Step 4**: You can now:
- Use the SWP (Systematic Withdrawal Plan) calculator

- Plan your retirement withdrawals
- Analyze long-term performance

Once imported, you can:

1. **Modify the Portfolio**: Add or remove funds, adjust weightages
2. **Use Calculators**: Run calculations with your investment parameters
3. **Compare Results**: See how the bucket performs with different strategies
4. **Save for Later**: Your selections remain until you change them

## 7. Performance Metrics Explained

### 7.1 Rolling Returns

**What it is**: Rolling returns calculate the return for a specific period (e.g., 3 years) starting from different dates. Instead of one 3-year period, you get multiple 3-year periods.

**Why it matters**: Shows consistency and volatility of returns over time.

**Example**: If a fund has a mean rolling return of 12%, it means on average, any 3-year period returned 12% annually.

### 7.2 CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate)

**What it is**: The annualized return rate assuming your investment compounds over time.

**Why it matters**: Provides a standardized way to compare different investments over different time periods.

**Example**: If â‚¹1,00,000 becomes â‚¹1,40,000 in 3 years, the CAGR is approximately 11.9%.

### 7.3 XIRR (Extended Internal Rate of Return)

**What it is**: Calculates returns considering the exact timing of cash flows (important for SIP investments).

**Why it matters**: More accurate than CAGR for investments with multiple cash flows at different times.

**Example**: For SIP investments, XIRR accounts for each monthly investment's timing.

### 7.4 Positive Periods Percentage

**What it is**: The percentage of time periods where the investment generated positive returns.

**Why it matters**: Indicates consistency. Higher percentage means more reliable returns.

**Example**: 85% positive periods means the investment was profitable 85% of the time.

### 7.5 Standard Deviation

**What it is**: A measure of volatility - how much returns vary from the average.

**Why it matters**: Lower standard deviation means more stable, predictable returns.

**Example**: A fund with 12% mean return and 3% standard deviation is more stable than one with 5% standard deviation.

### 7.6 Maximum and Minimum Returns

**What it is**: The best and worst returns achieved during the analysis period.

**Why it matters**: Shows the range of possible outcomes and helps assess risk tolerance.

**Example**: If maximum is 25% and minimum is -5%, you know the potential upside and downside.

## 8. Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: Are Suggested Buckets guaranteed to perform as projected?

**A**: No. The projections are based on historical performance data and are not guarantees of future returns. Past performance does not guarantee future results. Use these as guidance, not promises.

### Q2: Can I modify a bucket after importing it?

**A**: Yes! After importing, you can add or remove funds, change weightages, or adjust any parameters in the calculators.

### Q3: How often is the performance data updated?

**A**: Performance data is automatically recalculated every 5 days (if the server is not under heavy load). The system uses the latest NAV (Net Asset Value) data available.

### Q4: What if a bucket doesn't have enough historical data?

**A**: The system requires at least 3 years of historical data for accurate calculations. If a fund doesn't have sufficient history, it may not be included in certain calculations.

### Q5: Can I create my own buckets?

**A**: Currently, only administrators can create buckets. However, you can create custom portfolios in the Investment or Retirement Plan pages by selecting your own funds.

### Q6: What's the difference between "Investment" and "Retirement" buckets?

**A**: 
- **Investment buckets**: Focused on wealth creation and growth
- **Retirement buckets**: Optimized for long-term retirement planning
- **Both**: Suitable for either purpose

### Q7: How are the projected returns calculated?

**A**: Projections use the 3-year mean rolling return and apply compound interest over 3 years. The calculation assumes â‚¹1,00,000 initial investment.

### Q8: Can I see performance for different time periods?

**A**: The detailed performance report shows 3-year and 5-year metrics. The bucket card shows 3-year projections, with UI ready for 5-year view (coming soon).

### Q9: What happens if I import the same bucket twice?

**A**: Importing again will replace your current fund selection with the bucket's funds. Your previous selections will be overwritten.

### Q10: Are there any fees associated with using suggested buckets?

**A**: No. The Suggested Buckets feature is free to use. However, actual mutual fund investments may have expense ratios and other fees as per the fund house terms.

## 9. Best Practices

### 9.1 Choosing the Right Bucket

1. **Assess Your Risk Tolerance**
   - Low risk if you prefer stability
   - Moderate risk for balanced approach
   - High risk if you can handle volatility

2. **Consider Your Investment Goal**
   - Short-term goals: Choose lower risk buckets
   - Long-term goals: Can consider higher risk for better returns
   - Retirement: Use retirement-specific buckets

3. **Review Performance Metrics**
   - Check positive periods percentage (higher is better)
   - Review maximum and minimum returns
   - Consider standard deviation (lower = more stable)

4. **Understand the Fund Mix**
   - Review which funds are included
   - Understand the allocation percentages
   - Ensure it aligns with your investment philosophy

### 9.2 Using Buckets Effectively

1. **Start with Projections**
   - Review the projected returns on the bucket card
   - Understand the assumptions (â‚¹1,00,000, 3 years)

2. **View Detailed Performance**
   - Always check the detailed performance report
   - Review both portfolio and individual fund metrics
   - Check live returns for real-world scenarios

3. **Import and Customize**
   - Import buckets that match your goals
   - Adjust investment amounts to your capacity
   - Modify date ranges as needed

4. **Compare Multiple Buckets**
   - Import different buckets to compare
   - Use calculators to see how each performs
   - Choose the one that best fits your needs

### 9.3 Important Reminders

- **Past performance is not indicative of future results**
- **Diversification is important** - don't put all investments in one bucket
- **Review regularly** - market conditions change
- **Consult a financial advisor** for personalized advice
- **Understand the risks** before investing

### 9.4 When to Revisit Buckets

- **Market conditions change significantly**
- **Your risk tolerance changes**
- **Investment goals evolve**
- **Performance metrics deteriorate**
- **New buckets become available**

## 12. Glossary

**Bucket**: A pre-configured investment portfolio with multiple mutual funds

**NAV (Net Asset Value)**: The price per unit of a mutual fund

**CAGR**: Compound Annual Growth Rate - annualized return rate

**XIRR**: Extended Internal Rate of Return - accounts for cash flow timing

**Rolling Returns**: Returns calculated over rolling time windows

**Weightage**: The percentage allocation of each fund in a portfolio

**Risk Level**: Classification of investment risk (Low/Moderate/High)

**Positive Periods**: Time periods where investment generated positive returns

**Standard Deviation**: Measure of volatility in returns
